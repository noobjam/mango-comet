# Aura memory

Incident V3 is the product authority. A story is not a learned cluster: it is
one persistent local same-hazard exposure viewed for one crop. Weekly evidence
gets a `component_id`, linked components retain an `exposure_id`, and each
exposure/crop pair receives a stable `incident_id`. Crop stage is dynamic
context with zero identity weight.

Here, **causal** means prefix-safe/as-of: a published week uses no future
evidence. It does not mean causal inference. A story begins only when an exact
local footprint has adequate monitored/evaluable crop denominators and meets
persistent or policy-defined severe confirmation. WATCH evidence and
low-coverage preludes cannot silently establish a story. Recovery, unresolved
response, recurrence, data gaps, splits, and merges remain explicit lifecycle
states; none proves crop death or biological recovery.

The map trajectory is the exact union of significant grid-cell rectangles for
each published week. Never draw a centroid trail, arrow, interpolated hull, or
other mark that implies physical movement or propagation. Selected incidents
show age-banded historical footprints and a crop/stage/lifecycle arc.

Weekly publication is immutable and append-only. A new release must declare
either `--first-release` or a prior analytics release with
`--previous-incident-dir`; validation protects historical components,
exposures, incidents, memberships, lineage, and lifecycle rows from rewriting.
Stage coverage must pass both the global gate and the supported-crop gate.
The sole weekly exception is a prior max-week `data_censored_at_boundary` row:
only its state/closure/right-censor marker may reopen, while causal milestones
and counters remain frozen; window changes require supporting later weeks.

The 2025→2026 V2 archetype experiment remains failed diagnostic provenance:
38.01% holdout acceptance, 61.99% novelty, and 69.40% prototype non-overlap.
Its artifacts stay immutable, but its archetype ID is never story identity.
Optional V3 archetypes may tag only eligible completed stories and never drive
incident identity.

Incident V4 retains the V3 identity but separates three clocks: daily
weather-derived pressure, irregular acquisition-grain Sentinel-2 crop evidence,
and knowledge-gated weekly story checkpoints. Historical releases without
retained availability timestamps are explicitly diagnostic reconstructed
replays; operational releases require strict weather, stage, and spectral
availability timestamps. Echo rows only age the last usable S2 observation,
rejected/unknown-QA attempts cannot become references, and S2 evidence never
makes a missing weather day observable.

Serve only a completed `incident_viewer_v4_*` as `STORY_MAP_RUN_DIR` for the
dual-clock product. Static geometry is hydrated separately from compact daily
state, and field drilldown has distinct daily-pressure, S2-attempt, and weekly
story/stage lanes. Footprints and density representations are precomputed;
requests do not cluster or reconstruct stories. Both server and browser
response caches are byte-bounded, and the release must pass the VM latency
benchmark plus browser visual gates before presentation.

V4 motif learning is a downstream, review-gated descriptor layer over eligible
completed incidents. Discovery uses knowledge-time-separated and
lineage/exposure-purged train, calibration, and sealed holdout cohorts. Live
prefixes are maturity-matched by observed weather days and distinct usable S2
source dates, and may only be `pending`, `novel_unassigned`, or `tentative`.
An unreviewed motif never appears as a map-approved story label.

Never claim crop death, causation, hazard propagation, diagnosis, yield loss,
or agronomic validation from tracking or clustering alone.
